DMOST Targets

.

The DMOST sample is composed of 14 systems distributed equally between the star-forming regions of Taurus and Lupus. The disks around Very Low Mass Stars are typically fainter than their more massive counterparts around solar-like objects. Therefore, these objects were selected due to their continuum brightness (to ensure appropriate calibration and analysis), and also based on previous detections of 12CO emission at millimeter wavelengths.

As part of DMOST, we will study all the relevant outer disk properties of our sample, including their disk size, mass, height of emitting layers, and brightness temperature profile. This is the first time that these kinds of measurements will be possible for a sample of VLMS, as our program will almost double the ALMA time on source for nearby VLMS.
